Easy Passwords !

My suggestion on how to create seemingly random, easy to remember passwords.

A little history of my passwords.

I have administrator access to a large corporation’s network. I currently have at least 8 different work related account that need passwords.

I got tired of trying to find new password every 60 days. I do not want to write my passwords down. I needed a clue I could write down. That clue needed to have meaning only to me. My favorite example, had a clue of “trees”. The password was “P9awm2caB”. That was for the trees around my house: 9 Pines. Apple, Willow, Maple, 2 Cherry, Aspin and a Birch.

Over the years, it became harder to come up with good Clue/Password combos. I finally came up with the idea of patterns. All I would have to write down is a reference letter and the number of the pattern I used. A clue of “d1y3” could be for the password “esXcfyUi7j”. See patterns below. When I need a new password, I just choose a new reference letter for each pattern. Maybe “s1k3” which becames “waZxdkL;p>”. They look random, but they hold meaning to me. All I have to remember is a few patterns. I do not need a new pattern every time I needed to change my password. Every once in a while, I will stop using one pattern and start a new one.

Creating Patterns.

  • Define a grid representing your keyboard. I use the grid below.
  • Each pattern should consist of 3-5 presses.
  • Decide where the reference character is on the pattern. Is it one of the presses or not? In Pattern 1 the zero represents an un-pressed reference key. Otherwise use press 1 as the reference.
  • Decide and mark which press or presses will be shifted. The bold numbers represent shifted presses.

Additional options.

  • Patterns could be split. Meaning after a certain key press you insert the next pattern. See pattern 2 press 2. You would make presses 1 and 2. Do the next pattern. Finish with presses 3, 4 and 5.
  • One or more presses could change. i.e. in pattern 1 the whole pattern could rotate around the reference key.
  • A press could be a particular key. i.e. the fourth press of the pattern is always the ‘z’ key.
